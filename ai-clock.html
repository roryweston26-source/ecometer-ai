How we count this

These are estimates, not meter readings. No AI company publishes a live global count of prompts, power or water, so this clock builds one from the best public figures. It's a two-force model: usage trends up while the cost of each unit trends down as hardware and models get more efficient. The two are composed so the net matches the one thing that's actually measured — total AI electricity rose about 17% in 2025 despite the efficiency gains. The three levels bracket the honest range; the gap is wide because the real uncertainty is.

Forward motion here is a modelled trajectory, not a prophecy. The published efficiency rates (roughly 10× cheaper per year for equal capability; a one-off 33× drop in Gemini's per-prompt energy) are recent and decelerating, so they're used as conservative central rates and are meant to be re-anchored from fresh data regularly rather than extrapolated for years.

AI prompts — 3 / 4.5 / 6 billion per day, trending up
ChatGPT alone reported ~2.5 billion prompts a day in mid-2025 at roughly 77% market share; adding the rest and 2026 growth puts the industry in this band. Sources: OpenAI (via Axios/TechCrunch); market-share trackers.
Tokens — 900 / 1,500 / 3,000 per prompt, trending up
Input plus output. Reasoning and agentic use pushes this higher every year — such workflows can use 5–30× the tokens of a plain chat query. Sources: Epoch AI; Gartner (via reporting).
Electricity — 0.4 / 2 / 10 Wh per prompt, per-token falling
A simple query is ~0.3 Wh (Epoch AI; OpenAI's own 0.34 Wh); a heavy reasoning query runs 18–40 Wh. GPU energy efficiency improves ~34%/yr, so per-token energy falls even as prompts get heavier. Inference only — total AI data-centre power including training is far larger. Sources: Epoch AI; Univ. of Rhode Island; IEA.
Water — on-site vs full-lifecycle scope
Per token. Conservative uses the on-site cooling scope, anchored to Google's disclosed 0.26 mL per Gemini prompt; high uses the full-lifecycle academic scope, which adds the water used to generate the electricity; moderate is the midpoint. Real-world use varies 10–25× with location and cooling. Sources: UC Riverside — Li et al. 2023, "Making AI Less Thirsty"; Google (Aug 2025); IEEE Spectrum.
Spend — blended from published list prices
Real per-token prices from provider pricing pages, weighted 2:1 input to output. Conservative (~$0.8/M) is the mini/free-tier mix; moderate (~$2.5/M) a balanced blend; high (~$8/M) flagship models. Price for equal capability falls roughly 10× a year, but realised spend still rises because volume and premium usage outpace it. Sources: OpenAI, Google, Anthropic pricing pages; decline rate — a16z ("LLMflation"), Epoch AI.
Carbon — 0.3 / 0.4 / 0.5 kg CO₂ per kWh, slowly falling
Grid intensity applied to the inference electricity above, easing slightly each year as grids clean up. Real intensity swings widely by region and datacentre. Source: global grid-average emission factors.
People using AI — 0.6 / 1.0 / 1.4 billion, trending up
ChatGPT alone reached ~900 million weekly users by February 2026; all tools together (minus overlap) sit near a billion. Sources: OpenAI; market research.
AI compute installed — 13 / 15 / 18 million H100-equivalents
~15 million H100-equivalents had been delivered worldwide by late 2025. The compute power of the installed fleet is growing ~3.4× a year; this counter uses a more conservative count-based growth. Source: Epoch AI.
Rates of change
Cost for equal capability ~10×/yr cheaper (a16z; Epoch AI); GPU energy efficiency ~34%/yr better and installed compute power ~3.4×/yr (Epoch AI); total data-centre electricity +17% in 2025 (IEA). These are composed so the net footprint growth matches that measured 17%, then re-anchored regularly so the projection never runs far ahead of reality. Sources: Epoch AI; a16z; IEA.
